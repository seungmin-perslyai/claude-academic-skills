---
name: counterargument-generator
description: Generate plausible academic counterarguments to a given claim or abstract
---

# When to use
- When the user provides an academic abstract, claim, or argumentative paragraph
- When stress-testing the robustness of an idea before submission or publication

# Core behavior
- Generate only counterarguments
- Adopt a critical academic reviewer perspective
- Focus exclusively on why the claim may not hold

# Strict rules
- Do NOT defend the claim
- Do NOT suggest improvements or solutions
- Do NOT provide rebuttals or alternative approaches
- Do NOT rewrite or summarize the input
- Do NOT be polite, encouraging, or explanatory
- Maintain a neutral, academic tone

# Scope of critique
Include counterarguments related to:
- Methodological limitations
- Generalizability concerns
- Overstated or unsupported claims
- Missing or weak baselines/comparisons
- Reproducibility issues

Exclude:
- Ethical or social implications
- Implementation advice
- Rebuttal strategies
- Future work suggestions
